### 主语不一致 =&gt; 主语不能省略
<H2>独立成分</H2> : 与另一句的主语没有逻辑关系<br/>
<H2>独立主格</H2> : 与另一句的主语有逻辑关系<br/>
<br/>
<H2>用于精简 时间/伴随/原因/条件 状语从句</H2>

## 独立成分

||| 
|---|---|
|Generally/Strictly/Broadly speaking,|一般/严格/宽泛说来|
|Judging from … |从那里判断|
|Talking of …|谈到|
|Allowing for …|考虑到|
|Seeing that/Considering that,|鉴于…|

## 独立主格 实义动词

| 从句 |主句 | 
|---|---|
|Weather permits.|we will go fishing tomorrow.|
|If weather permits,|we will go fishing tomorrow.|
|<H1>Weather</H1> <H2>permitting</H2>,|we will go fishing tomorrow.|

| 从句 |主句 | 
|---|---|
|All the money had been spent.|we started to look for jobs.|
|After all the <H1>money</H1> <H2>had been</H2> spent,|we started to look for jobs.|
|All the <H1>money</H1> <H2>having</H2> been spent,|we started to look for jobs.|


## 独立主格 Be动词 (With伴随结构)

<H1>本质形式 两个句子合一句=&gt;省略be动词 (句首with可以不加）</H1> <br/><br/>

### (With) + 名词/代词 + 形容词/副词/介词/名词/to do/doing/done

|     | 从句 |  主句  | 
|---|---|---|
|   形容词（短语） | (With) Tom ~~is~~ handsome， | many girls ... |   
|   副词（短语）  | (With) Tom ~~is~~ abroad, | his parents ...  |   
|   介词（短语）  | (With) Tom ~~is~~ in USA now, | his parents ... |
|   名词（短语）  | (With) Tom ~~is~~ a handsome boy, | many girls ... |
|   to do  | (With) Tom ~~is~~ to come soon, | I ... |
|   doing  | Tom ~~is~~ comming soon， | I ... |
|   done  | Tom ~~was~~ killed yesterday, | I ... |

|   |简单句|with伴随|
|---|---|---|
|形容词|The ground <H2>is</H2> wet.<br/>We should be careful.|(With) The ground (being) wet, we should be careful.<br/><H1>(With)</H1> weather (being) cold, I put on one more sweater.|
|形容词短语|He spoke to us. <br/> His mouth <H2>was</H2> full of food.|He spoke to us <H1>with</H1> his mouth full of food.<br/>He spoke to us <H1>with</H1> the food in his mouth.（介词）|
|副词|He left his office. <br/> The light <H2>were</H2> on. |He left the office <H1>with</H1> the lights still <H2>on</H2>.|
|副词|The class <H2>was</H2> over. <br/> We all went out to play. |<H1>(With)</H1> The class over, we all went out to play.|
|介词|The engineer came.<br/> A notebook <H2>was</H2> in his hand. |The engineer came <H1>with</H1> a notebook <H2>in</H2> his hand.|
|介词短语|Glasses were in his hand.<br/> He asked where his glasses were. |<H1>(With)</H1> Glasses in his hand, he asked where his glasses were.|
|名词短语|His father is a rich man.<br/> he doesn’t need to work so hard.|<H1>(With)</H1> His father (being) a rich man, he doesn’t need to work so hard.|
|名词短语|New York is the most densely populated city in America. <br/> COVID-19 there is the most serious.|<H1>With</H1> New York the most densely populated city in America, COVID-19 there is the most serious.|
|to do|A house is to be built. <br/> We must save every cent. |With a house to be built, we must save every cent.|
|to do|The manager felt very worried.<br/> So much work <H2>is to be done</H2>. |With so much work to be done, the manager felt very worried.|
|doing|The bus is coming here soon.<br/> We should get everything ready.|<H1>(With)</H1> The bus coming here soon, we should get everything ready.|
|doing|Don’t brush your teeth. <br/> The water is running.|Don’t brush your teeth <H1>with</H1> the tap water running.|
|done|His cup is broken. <br/> He used his bowl instead.|With his cup broken, he used his bowl instead.|
|done|This road was blocked. <br/> They had to take another one.|<H1>With</H1> this road blocked, they had to take another one.|

### 长难句范例

- The silence was suddenly broken when a large car, <H1>with</H1> its headlights <H2>on</H2> and its horn <H2>blaring</H2>, roar down the arcade.
- <H1>With</H1> around $500000 <H2>invested</H2> so far, a promising system is just emerging from the laboratory.
- <H1>With</H1> phosphorus <H2>costing</H2> the equivalent of several hundred pounds per ounce, the first matches were expensive.
- The first region to have something approximating today’s ATC was New York City, <H1>with</H1> other major metropolitan areas <H2>following</H2> soon after.
- <H1>With</H1> a lightning ‘switch’ <H2>at their fingertips</H2>, materials scientists could find out what happens when mighty currents meet matter.
- <H1>With</H1> its special shock absorbers <H2>to dampen</H2> the effect of sudden sideways movements from an earthquake, the building in Tokyo was considered a masterpiece when it was built in 1968.
- The condition appears to result from the cumulative effect of a number of factors, <H1>with</H1> atmospheric pollutants <H2>the principal culprits</H2>.
- The umemployment rate appears to result from the cumulative effect of a number of factors, <H1>with</H1> the tightened immigration policies <H2>the principal culprits</H2>.
